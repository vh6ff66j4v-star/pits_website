The Gap in the Industry

In industrial automation, there's a recurring problem: a disconnect between what a client actually needs and what a supplier wants to sell.

Most providers default to a "one-size-fits-all" approach — systems that are easiest for them to build, often unnecessarily complex and costly for the end-user.

This leaves manufacturers trapped between two bad options: overpaying for features they'll never use, or struggling with generic systems that don't fit their workflow.
